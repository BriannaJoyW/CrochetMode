Cocoon Sweater

Materials Needed

  • Yarn color of your choice, 5 skeins (size 3 or 4)

  • Size G Hook (4mm)

  • Scissors

  • Yarn Needle

    • Stitch Tutorials

  • Chain Stitch (CH)

  • Half Double Crochet (HDC)

  • Slip Stitch (SS)

  • Double Crochet (DC)

  • Back Loop Only (BLO)

    • Pattern

  • Beginning: CH 123 (you can adjust this to any size you would like as it determines the length, this is just a base number from the ength I chose to make)

  • Row 1: DC in 4th CH from hook, DC across entire row, turn.

  • Row 2-99: CH 3 (count as first DC), DC BLO across entire row.

  • Row 100: CH 3 (count as first DC), DC BLO across entire row, finish off the rectangle by knotting off the yarn to secure it.

  • Arm Hole: Fold rectangle in half so your rows are running vertically, starting at the front use the yarn needle to stitch together the sides leaving a 10in gap on both sides (these are the arm holes).

  • Sleeves Row 1: (sleeves are created seperately then joined to the main piece so make 2 of these, they are also optional), CH 22

  • Sleeves Row 2: HDC in 3rd CH from hook, HDC across entire row.

  • Sleeves Row 3: CH 2 (count as first HDC), HDC BLO across entire row.

  • Sleeves Row 4: Repeat row 3 until work measures 20in, fold work in half and SS the sides together.

  • Finishing: Join sleeves to the sweater, you can either SC around the arm hole then SS the sleeve on or you can use the yarn needle to stitch the sleeve to the arm hole. Once finished weave in all ends and you are done!
